## The Russian doll of ads

Every major ad platform organizes ads in **3 levels**, like Russian nesting dolls:

```

  üìÅ Ad Set        ‚Üê Who sees it (targeting + schedule)
    üìÑ Ad          ‚Üê What they see (creative + text)
```

<Note>
  Different platforms use different names, but the structure is always the same:

  | Level | Meta | Google | TikTok | Apple |
  |-------|------|--------|--------|-------|
  | Level 1 | Campaign | Campaign | Campaign | Campaign |
  | Level 2 | Ad Set | Ad Group | Ad Group | Ad Group |
  | Level 3 | Ad | Ad | Ad | Ad (keyword) |
</Note>

## Level 1: Campaign ‚Äî "What's the goal?"

The campaign is where you decide:
- **What do I want?** (installs, website visits, awareness)
- **How much am I willing to spend total?** (optional campaign budget)

Think of it as: "I want more app installs and I have $500 to spend this month."

### Common campaign objectives

| Objective | When to use it | You pay for |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| **App Installs** | You want downloads | Each install |
| **Traffic** | You want website/landing page visits | Each click |
| **Conversions** | You want specific in-app actions | Each conversion |
| **Awareness** | You want people to know about you | Impressions (views) |

<Tip>
  **For your first campaign, always pick "App Installs."** It's the simplest and most directly useful goal.
</Tip>

## Level 2: Ad Set ‚Äî "Who should see this?"

The ad set is where you get specific about your audience:

- **Who:** Age, gender, location, interests, behaviors
- **Where:** Instagram feed, Facebook stories, Google search, etc.
- **When:** Start date, end date, time of day
- **How much:** Daily budget for this specific group

### Example ad set setup:

```
Ad Set: "Young Fitness Enthusiasts"
  - Age: 18-30
  - Location: United States
  - Interests: Fitness, Running, Gym
  - Placements: Instagram Feed + Stories
  - Daily budget: $10
```

<Warning>
  A common beginner mistake: making your audience **too narrow**.
  If you target "25-year-old women in Brooklyn who like yoga AND matcha AND own a dog" ‚Äî your audience is like 47 people.
  Start broad, then narrow down based on data.
</Warning>

## Level 3: Ad ‚Äî "What do they see?"

The ad is the actual creative content users see:

- **Image or video** (the visual)
- **Primary text** (the main message)
- **Headline** (the bold text)
- **Call to action** (the button: "Install Now", "Learn More", etc.)

## The golden rule: test at the right level

```
Want to test different audiences?  ‚Üí Make multiple Ad Sets
Want to test different creatives?  ‚Üí Make multiple Ads within one Ad Set
Want to test different goals?      ‚Üí Make multiple Campaigns
```

This structure keeps things organized and lets you pinpoint exactly what's working.
